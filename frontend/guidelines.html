Guidelines for Filing a First Information Report (FIR)

What is an FIR?

A First Information Report (FIR) is a written document prepared by the police when they receive information about the commission of a cognizable offence such as theft, assault, fraud, or cybercrime. Under Section 154 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), it is the duty of the police to register an FIR.

Who Can File an FIR?

  • The victim of the crime
  • Any person who has knowledge of the incident
  • A witness to the incident
  • Any responsible citizen

Information Required

  • Your name, address, and contact details
  • Date, time, and place of the incident
  • Clear description of the incident
  • Details of the accused (if known)
  • Details of witnesses (if any)
  • Any supporting information or evidence

Legal Rights of the Complainant

  • You have the right to receive a free copy of the FIR
  • Police cannot refuse to register an FIR for a cognizable offence
  • If refused, you may approach the Superintendent of Police or a Judicial Magistrate

Online FIR (e-FIR)

Many state police departments in India allow online complaint or FIR registration, especially for cyber crimes, lost documents, and minor offences. This portal is an educational and demonstration system and does not replace official government portals.

Government References (India)

  • Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), Section 154
  • Indian Penal Code (IPC) / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)
